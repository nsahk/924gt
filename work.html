Upgrade dampers (shock absorbers) 2007, 2011

Bilstein Sport dampers on front and rear, details can be found on the Specifications page. I didn't notice any difference when the rears were changed but the car definitely felt firmer with new front dampers. I replaced the front suspension bushes with polyeurethane ones at the same time as the dampers. Work undertaken by garage and parts from 911 Design and Bilstein.

Upgrade front brakes 2007, 2011

Braided brake hoses, grooved Black Diamond brake discs, EBC Yellow Stuff pads (not fitted yet, still using OEM Porsche pads). All bought from Motorsportworld.co.uk

Of these, I fitted only the new brake discs and pads myself. This took about six hours for the nearside and two for the offside because I'd never done it before. Loosening rusty bolts took the most time, but removal of the old disc and installation of the new is fairly straightforward with the Haynes manual. There are a few steps however where Haynes doesn't explain what you need to do and I'd say that if bolts are inaccessible, take the time to ensure you can't do a job in a different order. I will post some pictures to explain this when I find the time.

I bought grooved discs after reading some negative comments about cross-drilled discs. As with many car related matters, the internet provides as many people for something as against.

I have not fitted the new EBC Yellow Stuff pads because when I replaced the front discs I wasn't able to manually push the brake calipers back into the pistons and didn't have a tool to do the job. I have since bought a G clamp and will approach this job when the existing pads run down.

When installing the brake discs, I removed the front brake disc shields, which were rusty but servicable. This was a simple matter of removing a few bolts. Each one weighs about 250g and after asking a few "experts" I decided they weren't needed.

Upgrade front suspension 2011

When changing the front brake disc I saw that the wishbones looked very rusty and having often read how new front bushes "transformed" the handling of cars I decided to replace the rubber bushes with polyeurethane ones.

I left the car with Auteg Motorsport in Nurburg, Germany to have the work done. I can say that the car feels different, the front feels harder and it doesn't crash around as much over rough road surfaces. However, I asked for toe-out to be added at the front within Porsche specifications, but I might have this removed because it makes the car wander in a straight line too much for my liking.

The garage that did the work bought the parts for me but from memory they were: €80 for wishbones, €100 for bushes, €200 for Bilstein sport dampers
